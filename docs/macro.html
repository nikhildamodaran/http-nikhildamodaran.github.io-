What is Repo Rate?

Repo rate, also known as policy rate or repurchase rate, is the rate at which RBI lends money to banks. When the central bank reduces the repo rate, banks get money at a cheaper rate, and in turn, they reduce interest rates for borrowers. This is similar to the Repo rate for US Treasury and primarily a short term lending instrument with the terms of repurchase decided at the time of borrowing.

RBI recently (October 1, 2019) announced a <lending rate which would be linked to this repo rate, and hence makes repo rate an active interest rate which could determine credit supply in the market.

What is Reverse Repo Rate?

is the rate at which RBI provides interest on deposits with the RBI to commercial banks. albeit for shorter duration of deposit.

How reverse repo rate became benchmark interest rate in the economy Udit Mishra, Indian Express.

An important clarification is required - Reverse repo rate can only be maintained for short term, and hence it is different from Interest on Reserves for commercial banks by the Fed.
